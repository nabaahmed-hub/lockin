Slow responders need progressive overload through reps before weight. Hit the top of your rep range for 2 sessions in a row → then add weight. Never jump mid-session. Consistency over intensity every time.
The 3-second eccentric rule
Every lowering phase of every exercise is 3 seconds. This is the single biggest change from your previous training. Time under tension — not just load — is what creates the lean, defined look in bodies that change slowly. It also reduces injury risk on your knees by controlling the load rather than dropping into it.

Tofu guide — how to make it actually good
Pressing is non-negotiable. Skip it and you get soft, soggy tofu. The water needs to come out for it to crisp properly.
Press 400g firm tofu between towels · heavy pan on top · 30 minThis is the step most people skip. Don't.
Cut into 2cm cubes · toss in marinade2 tbsp soy + 1 tsp sesame oil + 1 tsp garlic powder + ½ tsp smoked paprika + 1 tsp cornstarch
Bake 400°F (200°C) · single layer · flip at 15 min · total 25–30 minShould be golden and crispy on all sides. Don't crowd the tray.
Cool + store airtight · keeps 4 daysReheat in pan or air fryer 3–4 min to re-crisp. Never microwave — goes soft.
